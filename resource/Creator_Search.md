### What is this?
This guide explains the two primary strategies for creator discovery in Vibe: starting with a broad, exploratory search versus using a highly specific, descriptive AI prompt.

### Why is it useful?
Understanding when to use each approach is key to an efficient workflow. There is no single "best" way; the right strategy depends entirely on your immediate goal. Using the right approach for the right task saves time and leads to higher-quality results.

### When to Use a Broad Search & Filters (The "Exploratory" Approach)
This method is best when you are in the early stages of planning and are not yet sure of the specific creator profile you need.
* **Goal:** To understand the landscape and discover possibilities.
* **Example:** You have a new campaign idea but want to see the variety of creators available before finalising your brief. You might start with a broad search like `"sustainability creators"` and then use the filters to explore different follower tiers, locations, and engagement rates to see what is possible.

### When to Use a Specific, Descriptive Prompt (The "Efficiency" Approach)
This is the most powerful and efficient method when you have a clear, well-defined creator brief.
* **Goal:** To get the most relevant results in the fastest possible time.
* **Example:** Your brief specifies "US-based vegan skincare creators with 10k-50k followers on TikTok". You should use a single, descriptive prompt that includes all of these criteria to get a precise, high-quality shortlist immediately.

### Pro-Tips
* For maximum efficiency, always default to a specific, descriptive prompt if you know what you are looking for.
* Use the exploratory, filter-based approach when you need to gather data to build your initial campaign strategy.
